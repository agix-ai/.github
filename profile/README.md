**An operating system for teams of AI agents you own and run yourself.**

```sh
brew tap agix-ai/agix
brew install agix-aos
```

AI can answer your questions. What people actually strive for is to **create** — to build something they love that came out of their own head. Agix exists for that. Instead of renting a single assistant, you keep a **hive**: a governed team of specialist agents on infrastructure *you* control — advocates that want you to succeed, and adversarial enough to correct you when you're wrong.

**Our mission: help people install an AI foundation they own and grow — not features they rent.**

### Why Agix?

- **You own it.** Runs on your machine through the coding-agent CLI you already use — no API key required to start, no telemetry, nothing phones home. Your agents, your data, your knowledge never leave your control.
- **It's governed.** The agent that does the work is never the agent that verifies it, and every action traces back to a person. A fleet you can actually trust with real work — not a black box.
- **It compounds.** What the hive learns lands in the **Comb** — a knowledge graph you own. What it learns on a Tuesday it still knows in March. An old hive knows more than a new one, and it can't be copied.
- **One conductor.** You talk to one agent; it fans the work out to the fleet and brings back what matters. One long conversation instead of nine parallel ones.

### How we're organized

Agix is three efforts under one roof — an open core, a research lab that keeps it honest, and the implementation work that proves it in production:

| | |
|---|---|
| **Agix AOS** — *the open core* | A local agentic operating system and agent development kit: the `agix` CLI, a fleet of legible specialist agents, an intra-agent message bus, and the Comb. Apache-2.0. *(Open-sourcing now — landing here as `agix-aos`.)* |
| **[homebrew-agix](https://github.com/agix-ai/homebrew-agix)** — *distribution* | The official Homebrew tap. One `brew install` and the hive is on your machine. |
| **The lab** — *research* | A standing research operation that runs nightly: live comparative studies, reproducible benchmarks, and distillation experiments — the adversarial proving ground where we try to break our own system before you do. Studies publish here as they clear review, negative results included. |
| **[Agix](https://agix-ai.io)** — *implementations* | Agentic implementation work, for teams that want the foundation designed and built *with* them. The field work funds the open work — and what we learn there flows back into the OS. |

### What the lab is testing

The hypothesis behind all of it: **a governed, verified body of knowledge — continuously re-rendered onto the current generation of small models — holds a durable lead on *your* domain at a fraction of frontier cost.** The corpus appreciates; any single model depreciates. And verification is the frontier: generation is commoditized, while knowing — cheaply, and with receipts — that agent work is *right* remains the hard, valuable problem. That's where we point our research, and we publish what we find either way.
